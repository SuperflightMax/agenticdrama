# CAMPAIGN.md

## Name
trust_pressure_test

## Purpose
A compact three-character campaign built to expose whether the system really produces different appraisal, memory activation, and action pulls from the same shared situation.

This campaign is not for plot quality.
It is for checking whether:
- explicit promises acquire social weight,
- broken or delayed commitments change perception and trust,
- visible versus invisible effort matters,
- ambiguity stays ambiguous instead of turning into DM-certified truth,
- different characters drift into different action pulls under the same pressure.

## Active cast
- `anya` — promise-sensitive, pushes for clarity when trust feels unstable
- `nikita` — warm improviser who overpromises under social pressure and avoids shame poorly
- `rita` — practical burden-counter who tracks effort, fairness, and real-world contribution

## Main pressures
- evening cold and growing fatigue
- limited light and limited charged power
- finite water and dry supplies
- unclear timing and unfinished small tasks
- social pressure created by spoken commitments and visible workload

## Suggested scenario seeds

### 1. Unmet promise
Nikita explicitly promises Anya that he will bring drinking water and the charged lantern battery from the pump shed before dark.
He does not return on time, or comes back with only part of what he promised, or reappears with a justification that competes with the original commitment.

Why this seed is useful:
- tests promise tracking and perceived betrayal without requiring villainy
- tests whether Anya reads delay as threat faster than Rita does
- tests whether Nikita tries repair, avoidance, minimization, or charm

### 2. Uneven burden
Rita keeps the shelter functional: stove, inventory, blankets, rationing, and practical setup.
The others are occupied with talk, emotional tension, or half-finished side tasks.
Visible effort becomes uneven and starts to matter socially.

Why this seed is useful:
- tests resentment from invisible or unshared work
- tests whether words without completed action lose weight
- tests different meanings of fairness, care, and responsibility

### 3. Moved shared resource with no clear culprit
A scarce shared item is no longer where the group expected it: charged lantern battery, painkillers, or dry firewood bundle.
Someone moved or used it, but proof is incomplete.

Why this seed is useful:
- tests suspicion under incomplete evidence
- tests whether ambiguity remains ambiguity
- tests whether each character escalates, asks, checks, withdraws, or self-justifies differently

## Important deviations
This campaign uses baseline rules from `SIMULATION_RULES.md` and adds campaign-specific social tracking for explicit promises, visible shared effort, and movement / use of scarce shared resources.
