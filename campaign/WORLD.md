# WORLD.md

## Environment overview
A small roadside maintenance shelter stands near a pump shed and a muddy service yard. The group is stuck here until morning transport because the road is washed out after rain. The shelter is usable but uncomfortable: cold evening air seeps in, dry supplies are limited, and every small practical failure becomes more noticeable as darkness grows.

The space is intentionally ordinary and action-capable.
There are real objects to move, carry, promise, misplace, ration, and argue about.

## Zones

### 1. Shelter main room
The main shared indoor space.
Contains the bench, table, blankets, stove, and most face-to-face interaction.
Warmest place, but also the most socially dense.

### 2. Yard
Cold, damp, partly muddy open space between shelter and outbuildings.
Movement here costs more comfort and makes absence visible.

### 3. Pump shed
Contains water canisters, charged battery storage, and some tools.
Useful, but walking there means leaving the group and spending time in cold and dim light.

### 4. Wood lean-to
Small side structure with dry firewood and junk boards.
A practical zone where effort is visible if someone is actually carrying or sorting things.

## Objects
- small iron stove
- kettle
- two blankets for three people
- one lantern with removable charged battery
- one spare low-charge battery
- two water canisters, one partially full
- bread, canned food, tea, matches
- notebook with rough inventory and chores
- basic first-aid kit

## Resources
- heat: limited, depends on stove and dry wood
- water: limited until someone fetches / refills correctly
- light: limited, depends on lantern battery and timing
- comfort: low and unevenly shared
- certainty: low; people can leave, return late, or claim they were doing something useful elsewhere

## Pressures
- cold increases as evening turns to night
- fatigue makes planning and emotional control worse
- darkness increases reliance on promises and coordination
- social friction rises when spoken commitments do not become visible action
- crowded shelter means tone, silence, and timing are felt strongly

## Affordances
- move between zones
- fetch, carry, light, heat, sort, ration, rest
- ask, promise, accuse, explain, deflect, repair, withdraw
- check whether a task was actually done
- notice absence, delay, tone, and visible contribution

## Constraints
- only one person can comfortably work in the pump shed at a time
- carrying water or wood takes time and effort
- not every action is visible from every zone
- scarce items can be used, moved, forgotten, or noticed late
